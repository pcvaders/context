On the Canon R5C, here's how to set the self-timer / drive mode:

**Quick method (the Q menu):**
1. Make sure you're in **Photo mode** (use the photo/video switch on top of the camera, not video).
2. Press the **Q button** on the back.
3. Navigate to the **Drive mode** icon.
4. Select one of the self-timer options:
   - **Self-timer: 10 sec** (the clock icon)
   - **Self-timer: 2 sec** (good for tripod shots to avoid shake)
   - **Self-timer: Continuous** (10 sec, then fires a set number of shots)

**Via the main menu:**
- Go to the **Shooting menu (red camera tab)** → look for **Drive mode**, then pick your self-timer setting.

A couple of notes:
- The R5C separates photo and video into distinct modes via the physical switch, so self-timer drive options only appear when you're in photo mode.
- For the 2-second timer, the camera also raises the mirror... well, the R5C is mirrorless, so it just adds a short delay—handy on a tripod to eliminate any vibration from pressing the shutter.
