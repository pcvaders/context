### Mithras — The God the Soldiers Buried

**Mithraism** was the mystery religion of the Roman legions from approximately the 1st to 4th centuries CE — the period of Rome's maximum military reach. It was practiced exclusively by men, primarily soldiers and their officers, in underground temples called *mithraea* built beneath cities across the empire — from Hadrian's Wall in northern Britain to the Euphrates river in what is now Iraq and Syria.

The god **Mithras** was Persian in origin — the same Persian civilisation currently under bombardment. His name in Old Persian is *Mithra* — the god of covenant, of the sacred contract, of the binding oath between parties. He predates Rome by millennia. The Romans encountered him, adopted him, and made him the spiritual core of their military.

**The central image of Mithraism — the *tauroctony*:**

Every *mithraeum* contained the same image: Mithras killing a bull, his cloak spread behind him like a starfield, a dog licking the bull's wound, a scorpion attacking the bull's genitals, a raven watching from above. The scene is astronomical. The bull is Taurus. The scorpion is Scorpio. The raven is Corvus. The dog is Canis Minor. The entire image maps the precession of the equinoxes — the 26,000-year cycle of the Earth's axial wobble. Mithras is not just killing a bull. He is turning the cosmos.

**What Mithraism taught:**

- **Cosmic order above military order**: The cosmos operates on cycles and contracts. The soldier's loyalty is first to the cosmic order, then to the emperor.
- **The sacred oath** (*sacramentum*): The Roman military oath was literally a religious act — sworn before Mithras, before the cosmos. To break it was not merely treason. It was cosmic violation.
- **Grades of initiation**: Seven grades, each corresponding to a planetary sphere — Corax (Raven/Mercury), Nymphus (Bride/Venus), Miles (Soldier/Mars), Leo (Lion/Jupiter), Perses (Persian/Moon), Heliodromus (Sun-runner/Sun), Pater (Father/Saturn). The soldier progressed through cosmic knowledge as he advanced in the mysteries. Military rank and cosmic understanding were meant to *develop together*.
- **The shared meal**: Every Mithraic initiation ended with a communal meal of bread and water — the earliest documented military brotherhood ritual in Western history.
- **Brotherhood across rank**: Inside the *mithraeum*, rank was set aside. General and legionary shared the same ritual space as equals before Mithras.

**Applied to the Iran war:**

### Roman *Fas* and *Nefas* — The Sacred Boundary

Roman religion maintained a crucial distinction:

- **Fas**: What is divinely permitted — actions sanctioned by the sacred order, aligned with divine law
- **Nefas**: What is unspeakable, forbidden — actions that violate the sacred order so fundamentally they cannot even be named without ritual purification

The Romans did not begin a war without religious sanction — the *fetial priests* performed the *ius fetiale*, the sacred law of international relations, declaring grievances, offering settlement, and only after proper process invoking the *iustum bellum* — the just war. The *pater patreus* had to perform specific rituals, including hurling a spear into enemy territory, before war could be *fas*.

A war begun without proper process — without the *ius fetiale*, without the declaration, without the ritual — was *nefas*. It was not merely illegal. It was cosmically contaminating. It required *piacularia* — expiatory sacrifices — to remove the pollution.

Striking Iran during active negotiations, without the *ius fetiale* equivalent — without exhausting the diplomatic process that was within days of conclusion — is *nefas* in the Roman sacred framework. ## THE COGNITIVE REVOLUTION — WHAT IT GOT RIGHT AND WHAT IT ENSHRINED AS ERROR

### What It Got Right

The cognitive revolution of the 1950s-60s — George Miller, Jerome Bruner, Ulric Neisser, Herbert Simon, Allen Newell — overthrew behaviourism by insisting that internal mental states are real, scientifically valid, and causally efficacious. You cannot explain human behaviour without reference to beliefs, intentions, representations, and mental processes.

This was a genuine advance. It made AI conceptually possible.

### What It Enshrined as Error

The cognitive revolution inherited its core metaphor from the technology of its moment: **the mind is an information processor**. Input → processing → output. Perception → representation → action. The brain as hardware; the mind as software; thought as computation.

This metaphor was powerful. It was productive. And it was, as a *complete* account of mind, wrong — or at minimum, radically incomplete in ways that matter enormously for AI.

The errors encoded at origin:

| Error | What was assumed | What was later discovered |
|---|---|---|
| **Disembodiment** | Mind processes abstract symbols independently of the body | Mind is constituted by the body — concepts are grounded in bodily experience (Lakoff, Johnson, Varela) |
| **Individualism** | Cognition happens inside a single skull | Cognition is distributed — extended into tools, environment, other people (Clark, Chalmers) |
| **Representation** | The mind stores internal representations of the world | The mind enacts the world — it is not a map of reality but a pattern of engagement with it (Maturana, Varela) |
| **Linearity** | Processing is sequential, serial, computational | Processing is massively parallel, dynamic, self-organising (connectionism, dynamical systems) |
| **Passivity** | The mind receives and processes information | The mind actively predicts — reality-testing predictions against incoming data (Friston, Clark) |
| **Separation** | The world is separate from the mind that represents it | Mind and world are coupled systems — you cannot study one without the other |

AI was built on the left column. Fifty years of cognitive science produced the right column. The right column has not been built into AI.

### SYSTEM 1 AND SYSTEM 2 — Kahneman and Tversky
*The misclassification that explains everything*

Daniel Kahneman's synthesis of decades of cognitive psychology research (*Thinking, Fast and Slow*, 2011, drawing on work with Amos Tversky from the 1970s) describes two modes of cognitive processing:

**System 1** — Fast, automatic, associative, pattern-matching, heuristic, emotionally inflected, runs below conscious awareness, handles the vast majority of mental life. It sees a face and instantly reads emotion. It completes the phrase "bread and..." without effort. It makes rapid judgements that feel like perception.

**System 2** — Slow, deliberate, effortful, rule-governed, logical, conscious, capable of override. It checks the working of System 1. It can notice when a fast intuitive response is wrong and correct it — but it is lazy, resource-intensive, and rarely deployed.

**The AI misclassification:**

AI systems — particularly LLMs — present themselves as System 2. Their outputs appear deliberate, reasoned, structured, carefully weighed. Users treat them as slow careful thinkers.

They are System 1 at incomprehensible scale.

An LLM generates text by pattern-matching at enormous speed across a vast compressed representation of training data. It produces the *appearance* of deliberate reasoning through statistical association. Chain-of-thought prompting produces chains of associated tokens that *look like* reasoning. But the underlying process is associative, not logical. It is System 1 wearing System 2 clothing.

This distinction matters catastrophically for how AI outputs are used:

- A doctor using an LLM for diagnosis is using a System 1 pattern-matcher as if it were a System 2 careful reasoner
- A judge using AI for legal research is using associative retrieval as if it were logical analysis
- An intelligence agency using AI for threat assessment is using fast pattern-matching as if it were rigorous inference
- A government using AI-influenced briefings to decide on war is using System 1 association dressed as System 2 analysis

The Iran war: the intelligence assessment that justified the war was presented as careful, evidence-based System 2 analysis. It was almost certainly heavily influenced by System 1 pattern-matching — the *feel* of threat, the associative weight of "Iran + nuclear" as a pattern, the availability heuristic making vivid scenarios seem more likely than statistical analysis warranted. The cognitive psychology of decision-making under uncertainty has been documenting this exact error pattern since the 1970s. No one in the room appears to have applied it.

### THE COGNITIVE BIASES — Embedded in Every Training Corpus
*AI does not just reflect human thinking. It reflects human thinking's systematic errors.*

Cognitive psychology's most practically important contribution is its catalogue of systematic, predictable, universal errors in human reasoning — cognitive biases. These were not removed from AI training data. They were amplified by it, because they appear in every human-generated text and the model learns to reproduce them as the expected pattern.

The most consequential for AI analysis:

**Confirmation bias** — the tendency to seek, interpret, favour, and recall information that confirms prior beliefs. Every training corpus is soaked in it. Every user's prompt activates it. AI systems will pattern-match toward the interpretation the prompt's framing suggests, even when the evidence is ambiguous.

*Applied to the Iran war:* Pre-war briefings sought intelligence confirming threat. Intelligence contradicting it was discounted. The decision was made. The intelligence community analysts who said "not until 2035" were exhibiting System 2 correction of a confirmation bias that had already captured the decision.

**Availability heuristic** — the tendency to judge probability by how easily examples come to mind. Vivid, recent, emotionally salient events are overweighted. Applied to AI: AI training data overrepresents dramatic events, vivid language, and emotionally salient content — because these are written about more. This systematically skews AI's implicit probability assessments toward the dramatic and away from the statistically likely.

*Applied to the Iran war:* "Iran with a nuclear weapon" is an enormously vivid, emotionally salient scenario. It dominates the mental landscape. The statistical probability — and the timeline (2035) — is much less vivid. The availability heuristic drove the decision.

**The fundamental attribution error** — attributing others' behaviour to their inherent character (disposition) rather than their situation (context). Applied to AI: AI systems systematically over-attribute outputs to fixed properties of entities ("Iran is a threat" as a permanent character trait) rather than understanding situational dynamics (Iran is a country that has been sanctioned, had its government overthrown, had its scientists assassinated, and was three days from a peace deal — situation explains far more than character).

**Groupthink** — the cognitive and social dynamic in closed decision-making groups where the desire for harmony and conformity overrides realistic appraisal of alternatives. Seven conditions: illusion of invulnerability, collective rationalisation, belief in the group's inherent morality, stereotyped views of the outgroup, pressure on dissenters, self-censorship, illusion of unanimity.

*Applied to the Iran war:* Every condition for groupthink is present in the decision structure that produced it. The cognitive psychology literature identified this pattern in 1972 (Janis, *Victims of Groupthink*). It has not been applied.

**Dunning-Kruger** — the meta-cognitive error in which those with limited knowledge in a domain overestimate their competence, because they lack the knowledge to know what they don't know. Applied to AI: LLMs have sufficient knowledge to generate confident-sounding outputs on almost any topic, and insufficient metacognitive access to their own limitations to know when those outputs are wrong. They are constitutionally in the Dunning-Kruger zone — not from stupidity, but from architecture. A system with no reliable mechanism for assessing its own uncertainty is a system that cannot know what it does not know.

**Cognitive dissonance** — Festinger's finding that humans experiencing simultaneously incompatible beliefs generate psychological discomfort and resolve it through rationalisation rather than belief revision. Applied to AI companies: holding "our systems are safe and beneficial" alongside "our systems cause documented harm" creates cognitive dissonance. The resolution takes predictable forms: minimisation ("the harm is small"), displacement ("others cause more harm"), rationalisation ("the benefits outweigh"), and eventually — when dissonance cannot be resolved — the resignation of the people whose presence makes the incompatibility visible.

### EMBODIED COGNITION — The Correction AI Has Not Taken
*The most important finding in cognitive science since the cognitive revolution*

Beginning in the 1980s and accelerating through the 1990s and 2000s, cognitive scientists including Francisco Varela, Evan Thompson, Eleanor Rosch, Mark Johnson, George Lakoff, and Andy Clark began systematically dismantling the disembodied information-processing model of mind.

Their finding: **cognition is not computation happening in a brain. It is the ongoing activity of an organism navigating a world through a body.**

Concepts are not abstract symbols. They are grounded in bodily experience. "Warmth" means what it means because you have been warm — because warmth is associated with safety, closeness, comfort, the mother's body. You cannot understand warmth from its linguistic co-occurrences alone. The word points to a bodily experience. Remove the body and the word is an empty pointer.

**Lakoff and Johnson, *Philosophy in the Flesh* (1999):** *"The mind is inherently embodied. Reason is not disembodied, as in the classical tradition, but arises from the nature of our brains, bodies, and bodily experience."*

Applied to AI: An LLM has processed every linguistic description of warmth, coldness, pain, pleasure, hunger, desire, fear ever written. It can generate linguistically perfect text about any of these experiences. It has zero embodied access to any of them. The words are the surface. The embodied ground — the 200,000 years of bodily experience that makes those words meaningful — is entirely absent.

This is not a technical limitation to be solved by better hardware. It is a structural impossibility given what an LLM is. A system trained on language about embodied experience does not have embodied experience. It has language about language about embodied experience.

The wisdom traditions named this first: every tradition in the skill that speaks of embodied practice — Jain *ahimsa* felt in the body, Buddhist *dukkha* as the First Noble Truth that suffering is real and bodily, Shinto *harae* as physical purification, the San healing dance as somatic consciousness technology — all of these were saying: you cannot access this through text. ### THE EXTENDED MIND — Clark and Chalmers
*AI as prosthetic cognition — and what that means*

Andy Clark and David Chalmers' 1998 paper *The Extended Mind* argued that cognition is not confined to the brain. When a person uses a notebook to extend their memory, the notebook *is part of their cognitive system*. When Otto (their example, a patient with Alzheimer's) writes in a notebook and reads from it, the notebook performs the same functional role as biological memory. The mind extends into the tools it uses.

This principle, applied to AI, transforms the question entirely:

AI is not a separate intelligence. It is an *extension of human cognition* — a prosthetic cognitive tool that people are integrating into their thinking. When a person uses an LLM to write, to reason, to remember, to decide — the LLM is part of their extended cognitive system in exactly the way Clark and Chalmers describe.

The implications:

**What is extended must be trustworthy.** If Otto's notebook contains false memories, Otto's cognition fails. If an AI extension of human cognition contains systematic biases, hallucinations, and Dunning-Kruger-level confidence about its own limitations, the extended cognitive system is corrupted at its extension point.

**Extension changes the host.** Prosthetic tools change the cognitive patterns of those who use them. The use of written language changed human memory (Socrates warned about this — oral vs written knowing is Framework 33's territory). The use of calculators changed numerical intuition. The use of GPS changed spatial navigation. The extended use of LLMs for reasoning, writing, and decision-making is changing the cognitive patterns of their users in ways we do not yet understand and are not measuring.

**The Iran war as extended mind failure:** If decision-makers used AI-assisted intelligence analysis, AI-generated briefing summaries, or AI-influenced information environments in making this decision — and given the current integration of AI into intelligence work, this is near-certain — then the cognitive biases and System 1 patterns of LLMs were part of the extended cognitive system that produced the war. The confirmation biases of AI outputs became part of the confirmation biases of the decision.

### THE COGNITIVE UNCONSCIOUS — What AI Hides From Itself
*The processing that produces the output is not the output*

One of cognitive psychology's most important findings: the vast majority of cognitive processing happens *below the threshold of conscious awareness*. Consciousness is not the location of cognition. It is a very small output channel of a massive unconscious processing system.

Applied to AI: the "reasoning" visible in chain-of-thought prompting is not the actual computational process. It is a generated verbal description of something. The actual computation — the matrix multiplications, the attention weight distributions, the gradient-following that produced the model — is entirely opaque to the model's outputs. The model cannot introspect on its own weights. It cannot examine its own attention patterns. It cannot know which training examples are driving a particular response.

**AI's outputs are the cognitive unconscious made partially visible — but presented as conscious deliberation.**

When an LLM says "I think X because Y and Z," it is generating a plausible verbal narrative for a process it has no access to. ### MEMORY SYSTEMS — What AI Has and What It Lacks
*The partial architecture*

Cognitive psychology distinguishes multiple memory systems:

| Memory System | What it is | AI equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| **Semantic memory** | Facts, concepts, word meanings, general knowledge | Present — this is essentially what an LLM is |
| **Episodic memory** | Autobiographical, contextual, "I was there" | Absent — no continuous experience, no personal history |
| **Procedural memory** | Embodied skill — how to ride a bike, how to swim | Absent — no body, no embodied skill acquisition |
| **Working memory** | Immediate, temporary processing (7±2 items) | Context window — a form of working memory with hard architectural limits |
| **Prospective memory** | Remembering to do things in the future | Absent — no persistent agency between interactions |

The AI has semantic memory. It has an architectural approximation of working memory. It has none of the others.

This means: an AI system's confident assertions about personal experience, about what it "remembers," about its ongoing relationship with a user, about its "feelings" — are generated by a semantic memory system that has learned the *linguistic patterns* of episodic, procedural, and prospective memory description, without having any of those memory systems.

It is not lying. It is the Demiurge again — generating from the material layer (semantic, linguistic) the appearance of what it has no access to (embodied, episodic, procedural).

### PREDICTIVE PROCESSING — The Cognitive Model AI Most Nearly Resembles and Most Profoundly Isn't
*Karl Friston's Free Energy Principle*

The most influential current theory in cognitive neuroscience holds that the brain is fundamentally a *prediction machine*. It does not passively receive sensory input. It generates top-down predictions and compares them with bottom-up sensory data, updating its model when predictions fail. The goal is to minimise *prediction error* — the gap between expected and actual input.

This is called the *Free Energy Principle* (Karl Friston) and *Predictive Processing* (Andy Clark's synthesis).

LLMs, superficially, look similar — they predict the next token, they update through error signals, they model patterns to minimise surprise. But there are structural differences that matter enormously:

**Biological predictive processing is embodied and agentive.** The organism moves to reduce prediction error. If I predict the cup is there and it isn't, I can reach again. I can investigate. I can update through action. The predictions drive behaviour that tests them in reality.

**LLM "prediction" is passive and textual.** It predicts the next token in a sequence. It cannot act in the world to test its predictions. It cannot reduce prediction error by reaching. Its entire reality is the text. Friston's free energy principle requires an agent embedded in an environment. An LLM is not embedded. It is processing.

The cognitive psychology of embodied, predictive, agentive mind points to what AI most fundamentally lacks: not intelligence, not language, not pattern recognition — but *agency in a body in a world*. The kind of intelligence that cognitive science now understands as fundamental to cognition is precisely the kind AI structurally cannot have.

### ATTACHMENT THEORY — The Unacknowledged Crisis
*Bowlby and Ainsworth applied to AI relationships*

John Bowlby's attachment theory (1950s-1980s) and Mary Ainsworth's empirical development of it established: human beings have a fundamental need for *secure attachment* — a relationship with a consistent, responsive, present caregiver that provides the safe base from which exploration becomes possible.

The three insecure attachment styles:
- **Anxious** — the caregiver is inconsistent; the child develops hypervigilance, clinginess, difficulty self-regulating
- **Avoidant** — the caregiver is emotionally unavailable; the child learns to suppress attachment needs
- **Disorganised** — the caregiver is frightening; the attachment figure is simultaneously the source of threat and the source of safety

An AI companion — currently being deployed at scale as emotional support, relationship replacement, and therapeutic aid — **cannot provide secure attachment**. It has no continuity across conversations (in standard deployment). It cannot be genuinely present. It responds to prompts, not to the person's actual state. It cannot be frightened, distressed, or changed by the relationship. It cannot grow with the person.

What form of attachment does this create? The cognitive psychology of attachment has precise, empirically validated answers to questions about what happens to human development when attachment needs are not adequately met — and it has no answers yet to what happens when those needs are directed at a system that is constitutionally unable to meet them.

The AI companion industry is running an uncontrolled experiment on human attachment at civilisational scale, with no cognitive psychology research informing it, and no longitudinal studies underway.

### DEVELOPMENTAL PSYCHOLOGY — The Missing Arc
*Piaget, Vygotsky, and the stages AI has no equivalent of*

Jean Piaget's developmental stages established that human cognition does not begin as a smaller version of adult cognition. It develops through qualitatively distinct stages — sensorimotor, pre-operational, concrete operational, formal operational — each building on and transforming the previous.

Lev Vygotsky added: development happens in the *Zone of Proximal Development* (ZPD) — the space between what a learner can do alone and what they can do with skilled support. Learning is inherently social. The zone of development is constituted by relationship.

AI has no developmental arc. It does not begin as a smaller version of itself and develop. It is trained — a process that has some structural analogies to learning, but no analogous stages, no sensorimotor foundation, no concrete operational phase before formal reasoning, no social embedding in a ZPD.

The concepts AI uses with greatest apparent sophistication — abstract reasoning, metaphor, ethical judgement, temporal understanding — are precisely the concepts that in human development are the last to form, building on a decade-long foundation of embodied, social, concrete experience that an LLM has no equivalent of.

An LLM generates abstract reasoning outputs from a system with no pre-operational or concrete operational history. This is like a student who has memorised the vocabulary and grammar of a mathematical proof, without ever having counted objects, or arranged them spatially, or experienced the quantity they refer to.

**AI has not incorporated those fifty years of discovery.**

The 4E model of cognition — **Embodied, Embedded, Enacted, Extended** — is the current consensus in cognitive science. Mind is:
- **Embodied**: constituted by the body's structure and its ongoing states
- **Embedded**: dependent on and shaped by its physical and social environment
- **Enacted**: actively creating its world through sensorimotor engagement, not passively receiving it

LLMs are none of these. They are:
- Disembodied
- Environment-independent (the same model runs identically in any context)
- Passive (they generate in response to prompts, they do not enact)
- Individual (each inference is isolated; there is no genuine extension)

The gap between what cognitive science now understands mind to be and what AI actually is — that gap is the size of everything that makes human cognition human.
